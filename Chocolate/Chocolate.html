What is Chocolate

Well, Chocolate is a food product that is from roasted and ground cacao pods (image provided), chocolate comes in a variety, it can come as liquid, solid, or a paste, on its own or as a 'flavoring agent' in other foods. It is used in many desserts like pudding, cakes, candy, ice cream, and Easter eggs. The harvesting method for chocolate (cacao seeds) are harvested by hand because machines could injure the trees, according to Cornell. Workers remove the pods, which are orange when they are ripe, and open them with a machete. Chocolate is the most popular sweet treat in the world. People around the world (but mostly in Europe and the United States) consume more than 3 million tons of cocoa beans a year, according to the World Cocoa Foundation.

This is a Theobroma Cacao Fruit, they are sourced from the Amazon basin where it grows in the shaded rainforest understorey and can reach 20 m in height.

The Health benefits of chocolate

Suprisingly, Several recent studies have examined the role that chocolate may have on heart health. Cacao beans are full of phytonutrients, which act as antioxidants and provide additional benefits. Furthermore, cacao beans are rich sources of iron, copper, magnesium, zinc and phosphorus, according to the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. Dark chocolate contains two to three times more beneficial flavanols than milk chocolate because milk chocolate's cacao concentration is diluted with milk and possibly more sugar.
